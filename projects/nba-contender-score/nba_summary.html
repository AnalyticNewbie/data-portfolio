NBA ContenderScore — Initial Evaluation

This analysis evaluates whether a commonly cited fan metric — the ContenderScore — meaningfully predicts a team’s likelihood of reaching the Conference Finals or better.

Research Question

Does the ContenderScore, a combined measure of offensive and defensive efficiency rankings, provide a statistically valid signal of championship contention?

Data & Methodology

  • Dataset: All NBA teams from 2005–2018 (30-team era)
  • Outcome variable: Reaching the Conference Finals or better
  • Model: Logistic Regression (binary classification)

The ContenderScore is defined as a scaled combination of offensive and defensive rank:

ContenderScore = 10 × ((30 − OffRank + 30 − DefRank) / 60)

This formulation assumes offense and defense contribute equally and linearly to contender probability — an assumption tested in later analysis.

Results

  • The ContenderScore is a statistically significant predictor of contender status (P < 0.001).
  • Overall classification accuracy: 89.5%
  • Each 1-unit increase in ContenderScore increases the odds of contention by approximately 3.3×.
  • Precision when predicting contenders: approximately 63%

Interpretation & Limitations

While the model performs well overall, its predictions are conservative.

  • Recall is low (36.5%), meaning many true contenders are missed.
  • The model excels at ruling teams out but struggles to identify “borderline” contenders.

This suggests that while the ContenderScore is a strong top-tier signal, its rigid structure limits its ability to capture nuanced paths to contention.

Follow-Up Analysis

  • Is offense or defense the stronger independent predictor?
  • Does removing fixed weights improve recall?
  • Do non-linear models better capture contender dynamics?
