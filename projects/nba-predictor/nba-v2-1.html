v2.1: Context-Aware Volatility

Refining uncertainty with Pace, Defense, and Form.

The Challenge

Version 2.0 was accurate, but its confidence intervals were static. It treated a chaotic, fast-paced Pacers vs. Warriors game with the same "margin of error" as a slow, defensive Knicks vs. Heat game.

In sports betting and analytics, knowing when you are uncertain is just as valuable as the prediction itself. I needed a system that could dynamically widen or narrow its prediction range based on the specific matchup style.

The Solution: Composite Sigma

I engineered a new "Composite Sigma" logic that adjusts the standard deviation of the prediction based on three external factors:

  • Pace Factor: Faster games (more possessions) = Wider prediction range.
  • Defensive Rating: Elite defenses "compress" the game, reducing variance.
  • Recency Bias (Form): A weighted look at the last 3 games to identify momentum shifts that the season-long model might miss.

SQL Engineering: The "Form" Window

To calculate "Form," I used SQL Window Functions anchored to the schedule. This allows future games (which have no stats yet) to look backward at the previous 3 completed games. 

-- Calculating "Heating Up" vs "Slumping"
AVG(g.home_pts - g.away_pts) OVER (
    PARTITION BY team_id 
    ORDER BY game_date_et 
    ROWS BETWEEN 3 PRECEDING AND 1 PRECEDING
) as rolling_margin_3
