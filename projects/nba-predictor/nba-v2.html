v2.0: SQL & ML Architecture

Moving from heuristics to trained Gradient Boosting models.

The Shift

v1.0 was a proof of concept, but it couldn't account for complex interactions like "Strength of Schedule." In v2.0, I moved the entire feature engineering pipeline into PostgreSQL and implemented scikit-learn pipelines.

1. SQL-First Engineering

Instead of slow Python loops, I created SQL Views. This allows me to calculate complex rolling averages and "Strength of Schedule" adjustments instantly at the database level.

2. Gradient Boosting

Switched to HistGradientBoostingRegressor. This handles non-linear relationships better than linear regression, capturing how fatigue impacts elite teams differently than average ones.
